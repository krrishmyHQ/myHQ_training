How Office Space Gets Transacted · Three Models, One Market
Model 01
Conventional
Leasing
Direct deal between a company and a building owner. The company takes an empty floor and runs everything themselves.
Long-term fixed commitment to the building
Fitout, furniture, IT — company's responsibility entirely
High upfront capital before one employee sits down
Best for stable, large headcounts with long planning horizons
Who it's for
Fortune 500 · Large GCCs · BFSI
→
Model 02
Managed Office
Enterprise Flex
An operator fits out the space completely and rents it to companies on monthly terms — fully serviced and day-one ready.
Pay per seat per month — everything included
Zero fitout cost · operational in days not months
Scale headcount up or down without renegotiating
OPEX not CAPEX — CFO's preferred structure
Who it's for
50–500 seats · GCC satellite · Hybrid enterprises
→
Model 03
Coworking &
On-Demand
Shared workspace booked by the day, week, or month — hot desks, dedicated desks, or small private cabins. No long-term commitment.
Shared amenities — meeting rooms, internet, reception
Lowest commitment, lowest entry cost — day passes available
Entry point for startups, freelancers, remote teams
High churn — but often the first step toward a managed office
Who it's for
Startups · Freelancers · Remote workers · 1–30 seats
→

Flex — Your Market · The Structural Shift
Flex Workspace · India 2024
Flex isn't a trend.
It's a structural shift.
No 5-year lock-in. No CAM negotiation. No ₹1,500/sf fitout bill. You walk in, plug in, and your team is operational in a day. That's the value proposition — and it's why 18% of India's office market is now flex.
22%
Flex Market Share 2024
Up from 9% in 2020 · JLL/CBRE/Knight Frank 2024
Use Case 1
Corporate & Enterprise
Satellite offices, project teams, GCC setup without 5-year commitment, hub-and-spoke real estate strategy. CFO-approved because it's OPEX, not CAPEX.
GCCs Enterprise 50–500 seats
Use Case 2
SMBs & Startups
First professional address. Infrastructure without capex. Grow from 5 to 50 seats without renegotiating a lease. The flex promise for growth-stage companies.
Series A–C 5–100 seats
Use Case 3
Individuals & Freelancers
Day passes, hot desks, monthly memberships. The original coworking promise — community, productivity, and escape from the home office. Still a core segment, but not the growth driver.
